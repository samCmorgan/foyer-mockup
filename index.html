Artist Spotlight

Beth Welch on Memory, Motherhood, and Making Art in Baton Rouge

March 6, 2026 5 min read By The Foyer Team
↳ Hero post photo

Beth Welch has been part of The Foyer since she first brought her drawings in three years ago. Since then, her work has evolved, her exhibitions have multiplied, and the questions she asks through her art have grown more layered. We sat down with her to talk about all of it.

Walk through the gallery on any given afternoon and you will likely find someone standing in front of one of Beth Welch's drawings for longer than they expected to. There is something about the work that holds you. It is not loud. It does not announce itself. But it stays with you after you leave.

Welch was born in Monroe in 1992 and studied abroad in Gorizia, Italy, before graduating from Louisiana Tech's School of Design in 2015. She currently works as the Exhibitions Coordinator at the Louisiana Art and Science Museum, and her own practice happens in the early mornings and late evenings, often after her children are asleep.

↳ Photo of Beth in the gallery

On the new series

Her ongoing series began with a single drawing she made as a new mother, trying to locate a particular memory from her childhood that felt suddenly urgent. She could not quite get to it. That gap, the space between what you remember and what actually happened, became the subject.

"Motherhood made me look backward," she told us. "I became very interested in the way children experience time, and how those early years compress and blur into something you can barely name. I wanted to find a visual language for that feeling."

"I became very interested in the way children experience time, and how those early years compress and blur into something you can barely name."
Beth Welch

The drawings are graphite-heavy, sometimes layered with collaged fragments or washes of diluted ink. Figures appear but rarely fully emerge. Rooms are recognizable but slightly wrong. The scale is deliberate: some pieces are intimate enough to hold in your hands, others large enough to walk alongside.

On showing work in Baton Rouge

Welch has shown nationally, from Portland to Dallas, and has been published in Create! Magazine. But she keeps coming back to Baton Rouge, and specifically to The Foyer, for reasons that have as much to do with community as commerce.

"This is where people actually live," she said. "I like that my work might end up in someone's home here. That feels like a different kind of conversation than a gallery show that closes after three weeks."

↳ Detail shot of Beth's work

What's next

She has two new pieces at The Foyer right now, both part of the ongoing series. A third is in progress. She is also working toward a solo show later this year, details to be announced.

In the meantime, if you want to see the work in person, it is on the floor. Which is, Beth would probably say, exactly where it belongs.
